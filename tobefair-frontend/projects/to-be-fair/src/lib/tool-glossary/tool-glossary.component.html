Tool Glossary


  • Importance of the Metric
  • Maturity
  • Maturity Computation
  • Scoring Mechanism
  • Importance of the Metric: We are using the definition presented by the RDA FAIR Data Maturity Model which may be:
    1. Essential: The Metric addresses an aspect with crucial importance to achieve FAIRness under most circumstances, i.e., the Digital Object will not be FAIR if it does not fulfill the metric.
    2. Important: The Metric "addresses an aspect that might not be of the utmost importance under specific circumstances, but its satisfaction, if at all possible, would substantially increase FAIRness".
    3. Useful: The Metric "addresses an aspect that is nice-to-have but is not necessarily indispensable".
  • Maturity: The maturity levels correspond to a simplified scoring system to indicate the FAIR compliance of a Digital Object. 2BFAIR follows the levels defined by F-UJI,which were inspired by the FAIRsFAIR CMMI-based three tiers. The F-UJI maturity levels are incomplete (0), initial (1), moderate (2), and advanced (3). For 2BFAIR, the maturity levels are defined as follow:
    1. Incomplete: The Digital Object does not address the FAIR Principles.
    2. Initial: Some initial characteristics are handled toward FAIR although they do not meet the Principles' definitions. There is intent to be compliant with FAIR principles and awareness of existing issues.
    3. Moderate: Limited FAIR characteristics are handled and there is focus toward full alignment.
    4. Advanced: Complete coverage of the FAIR Principles' characteristics aligned with organizational standards and practice.
  • Maturity Computation: The maturity level is calculated for principle as follows. In case of at least one metric has reached level 'initial', the corresponding principle cannot have a lower level even if all other metrics are scored lower. All levels above 'initial' are calculated as the rounded mean of all metrics scores for a given principle. The overall F-UJI FAIR level for a given research data object would analogously be calculated as a rounded mean of all principle scores.
  • Scoring Mechanism: The scoring mechanism defines how to compute the Metric's score. There are two types of computation: Alternative or Cumulative. In the alternative computation, Test results are considered independently, i.e., only one of the metric’s test can pass. The metric’s score then reflects the score of the test that passed. In the cumulative computation, the metric’s score is the total of the scores of all tests that passed.
